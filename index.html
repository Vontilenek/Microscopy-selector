Which microscope do I need?

Answer a few questions about your experiment and we'll recommend the best system for your needs.

What kind of sample are you imaging?
This is the biggest single factor in system selection.
🔬
Cells in a monolayer2D culture on glass or plastic — cells, slides, coverslips
🧫
Thick / 3D sampleOrganoids, spheroids, tissue sections, embryos (>10–15 µm)
🔷
Fixed slide / large areaIHC, FISH, whole tissue section — need to scan a big region
🫀
Live cells / dynamic processTime-lapse, tracking, fast events — cell viability matters
Question 2 of 6
Do you need optical sectioning (Z-stack)?
Optical sectioning removes out-of-focus blur from thick samples. For flat monolayers it's often unnecessary.
📐
YesNeed clean Z-slices, 3D reconstructions, or co-localisation across depth
📄
No / Not sureSingle focal plane, presence/absence, or basic fluorescence readout
Question 3 of 6
Is your sample live or fixed?
Live imaging has different constraints — speed, phototoxicity, and environmental control all become critical.
🟢
LiveCells are alive — time-lapse, dynamics, or short-term imaging
🔴
Fixed / StainedParaformaldehyde, methanol, or similar — cells are not alive
